1. Gamification & Motivation

The current "Spelling" screen shows a score and a bonus timer, which is a great start. To keep students coming back:

    Streaks & Milestones: Add a "Daily Streak" counter. Students (and adults!) are highly motivated by not breaking a chain of successful days.

    Avatar Customization: Allow students to use the points they earn to "buy" hats, colors, or pets for their profile.

    Progress Visualization: Instead of just "1/10," use a visual map or a "level up" bar so students feel they are moving through a world rather than just completing a list.

2. Accessibility & Learning Aids

For students, especially younger ones or those with learning differences:

    Audio Support: Add a "Speaker" icon next to the word. Hearing the word "Hello" while seeing the letters helps reinforce phonics and pronunciation.

    Visual Feedback: When a student picks a correct letter, it should flash green or "snap" into place with a satisfying sound. If incorrect, a gentle "wobble" or red flash provides immediate correction without being discouraging.

    Hint System: If a student is stuck for more than 10 seconds, highlight the correct letter in the pool below to keep the momentum going.

3. UI/UX Refinements

    Letter Contrast: In the third image, some letters (like the white 'H' on orange or white 'M' on purple) have good contrast, but ensure you meet WCAG standards for all color combinations to help students with visual impairments.

    Keyboard Support: If this is used on a PC/Chromebook, allow students to type the letters on their physical keyboard rather than just clicking. It builds muscle memory for typing.

    Language Toggle: I noticed a "KM" (Khmer) toggle in the top right. Ensure that if the language is switched, the instructions ("What's your name?", "Ready for some visual puzzles?") also translate to provide a fully localized experience.
